About the Survey

Project Overview

The Springs and Naula Survey was conducted on 8th October 2025 for mapping the traditional water source in the Kholsir Valley District Bageshwar Uttarakhand India. This initiative aims to map natural springs and historic naulas, which have served communities for generations.

Methodology

Our team conducted on-ground surveys using GPS technology to accurately record geographical coordinates, elevation data, and water discharge rates. Each location was photographed and documented with detailed metadata including Gram Panchayat information and physical characteristics.

Objectives

  • Document GPS coordinates and elevation of water sources
  • Measure water discharge rates for sustainable management
  • Create a digital archive of traditional naulas
  • Support naula and sping rejuvenation stratagies and plans for sustinable management of water resources
  • Preserve cultural heritage associated with these water sources

Survey Team

This survey was conducted by Dr J.S Rawat (Former National Geo-Spatial Chair Professor and Environmental Hydrologist/Geomorphologist) and Er Varun Rawat (Senior Software Engineer) in association with the team of Aman NGO, Gram Panchayats and local community members.
